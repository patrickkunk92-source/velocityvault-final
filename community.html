Ride Safe

Safety Essentials

The most important thing is getting home safe. Every ride, every time.

Helmet Always

A helmet reduces head injury risk by 85%. No ride is too short.

  • CPSC or ASTM certified
  • Replace after any impact
  • Replace every 3-5 years
  • Must fit snug, not loose

Be Visible

Most bike accidents happen because drivers don't see the rider.

  • Front white light (required)
  • Rear red light (required)
  • Reflective clothing at dusk
  • Use hand signals always

Road Rules

Bikes are vehicles. Follow the same rules as cars on the road.

  • Ride with traffic, not against
  • Obey all traffic signals
  • Use bike lanes when available
  • Yield to pedestrians

Theft Prevention

2 million bikes are stolen every year in the US. Protect yours.

  • U-lock through frame + wheel
  • Lock to immovable objects only
  • Register your serial number
  • Take photos for insurance

BMX Trick Guide

From beginner bunny hops to advanced aerials — learn the fundamentals

Beginner

Bunny Hop

The foundation of all BMX tricks. Lift both wheels off the ground simultaneously.

Key: Pull up handlebars first, then scoop the pedals to lift the rear. Practice on flat ground before obstacles.

Beginner

Manual

Ride on your back wheel without pedaling. Pure balance and body positioning.

Key: Shift weight behind the seat, arms extended. Use rear brake as your safety net. Start on slight downhill.

Beginner

180

Half rotation in the air, landing fakie (backwards). The first spin trick you'll learn.

Key: Carve into the spin, look over your shoulder where you want to go. Commit to the rotation fully.

Intermediate

Bar Spin

Spin the handlebars 360 degrees while in the air or on a manual.

Key: Practice throwing the bars while standing still first. Use your dominant hand to throw, catch with both. Gyro or detangler helps.

Intermediate

Tailwhip

Kick the frame around the handlebars while you stay in the air. Iconic BMX move.

Key: Get comfortable with big bunny hops first. Kick the frame with your back foot, catch the pedals with both feet.

Advanced

360

Full rotation in the air. The signature spin trick that separates intermediates from pros.

Key: Master 180s first. Need height and commitment. Carve hard into the spin, spot your landing early. Quarter pipes make this easier to learn.

Know the Law

E-Bike Classifications & Laws

Understanding e-bike classes helps you ride legally and avoid fines

Class 1

Pedal Assist Only

Motor: Up to 750W

Max Speed: 20 MPH (with pedaling)

Throttle: No

Where: Allowed on most bike paths, bike lanes, and roads. Most permissive class. No license or registration needed in most states.

Class 2

Throttle Assist

Motor: Up to 750W

Max Speed: 20 MPH (with or without pedaling)

Throttle: Yes

Where: Allowed on most roads and bike lanes. Some bike paths restrict throttle use. Check local regulations. No license needed in most states.

Class 3

Speed Pedelec

Motor: Up to 750W

Max Speed: 28 MPH (with pedaling)

Throttle: Optional (up to 20 MPH)

Where: Roads and some bike lanes only. NOT allowed on most multi-use paths. Helmet required in many states. Minimum age 16+ in some areas.

Note: E-bikes over 750W or 28+ MPH may require registration, insurance, or a license in some states. Our high-power e-bikes (1500W-3000W) may be classified differently — always check your local laws.

Plan Your Ride

Commuter Toolkit

Resources to make bike commuting easier and more enjoyable

Cost Savings

Average bike commuter saves $3,000-$10,000/year compared to car ownership. Gas, insurance, parking — it all adds up.

Health Benefits

30 min of cycling burns 200-500 calories. Regular riders have 46% lower risk of heart disease and improved mental health.

Weather Prep

Rain gear, fenders, and proper layering make year-round commuting possible. Waterproof panniers keep your stuff dry.

Eco Impact

Each mile biked instead of driven prevents 1 lb of CO2 emissions. A 10-mile daily commute saves over a ton of CO2 per year.
