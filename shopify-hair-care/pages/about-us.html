Where It All Began

Founded by Amara Okafor-Williams

CrownKinetics was born in 2019, in a small Brooklyn kitchen, out of frustration that turned into a revolution. Our founder, Amara Okafor-Williams, had spent over fifteen years searching for hair care products that truly understood her 4C coils. Instead, she found shelves lined with products loaded with sulfates, silicones, mineral oil, and ingredients she could barely pronounce — products marketed to Black women yet formulated with little regard for the unique science of textured hair.

After a devastating experience with chemical damage from a so-called "natural" product that left her with breakage and scalp burns, Amara made a decision: she would create the products she had been searching for her entire life. She enrolled in cosmetic chemistry courses, traveled to Ghana, Nigeria, and Jamaica to study ancestral hair care traditions, and spent eighteen months developing formulations that blended time-honored African and Caribbean botanical wisdom with cutting-edge trichological science.

The name CrownKinetics embodies our philosophy: your hair is your crown, and its movement — its kinetics — tells the story of where you have been and where you are going. Every coil pattern is a fingerprint. Every curl is a celebration. Every kink carries the memory of ancestral brilliance.

What started as five handcrafted products sold at a local farmers market has grown into a full line of luxury hair care trusted by thousands of women and men across the globe. But our mission has never changed: to give Black hair the scientific respect and cultural reverence it has always deserved.

“I did not start CrownKinetics to build a brand. I started it because I was tired of an industry that treated Black hair as an afterthought. Our hair is not difficult — it has simply been misunderstood.”
— Amara Okafor-Williams, Founder & CEO

Our Mission & Values

We exist to transform the relationship between Black communities and hair care — replacing damage with nourishment, shame with pride, and guesswork with science.

Rooted in Science

Every CrownKinetics formula is developed using peer-reviewed trichological research specific to textured hair. We study the structural differences of elliptical hair follicles, melanin distribution, and sebum production patterns to create products that work with your biology, not against it.

Cultural Reverence

We draw inspiration from centuries of African and Caribbean hair care traditions. From West African shea butter rituals to Jamaican black castor oil remedies, we honor the wisdom of our ancestors while advancing it with modern formulation science. Your crown carries history; our products protect that legacy.

Clean Integrity

Transparency is non-negotiable. We publish every ingredient, explain why it is included, and voluntarily submit to third-party testing. Our formulas are free from sulfates, parabens, phthalates, mineral oil, petrolatum, formaldehyde, and synthetic fragrances. If it cannot nourish your crown, it does not belong in our bottles.

Community First

CrownKinetics is more than a product line — it is a movement. We reinvest in the communities that inspire us through educational partnerships, cosmetology school scholarships, and by ensuring our supply chain directly supports women-led cooperatives across West Africa and the Caribbean.

Ethically Sourced

Every raw ingredient is traced from origin to formula. Our shea butter comes from a women-owned cooperative in northern Ghana. Our Jamaican black castor oil is traditionally roasted and cold-pressed in St. Thomas Parish. Our baobab oil is harvested sustainably in Senegal. Fair wages and ecological stewardship are built into every product.

Sustainability

Our packaging is crafted from post-consumer recycled materials and is fully recyclable. We use soy-based inks, biodegradable shipping materials, and carbon-offset delivery. By 2026, we are committed to achieving fully closed-loop packaging across our entire product line.

Our Clean Ingredients Promise

We believe what you put on your crown matters as much as what you put in your body. Every ingredient earns its place through proven efficacy and absolute safety.

What We Embrace

  • Cold-pressed, unrefined plant oils and butters
  • Organic botanical extracts and essential oils
  • Plant-derived proteins (rice, quinoa, baobab)
  • Raw, fair-trade shea butter and cocoa butter
  • Traditionally prepared Jamaican black castor oil
  • Fermented rice water and herbal infusions
  • Naturally derived preservatives (vitamin E, rosemary extract)
  • Sustainably harvested African botanicals
  • Low-pH, curl-friendly cleansing agents
  • Ingredients backed by trichological research

What We Refuse

  • Sulfates (SLS, SLES, ALS)
  • Parabens (methylparaben, propylparaben)
  • Silicones (dimethicone, cyclomethicone)
  • Mineral oil and petrolatum
  • Phthalates and synthetic fragrances
  • Formaldehyde and formaldehyde releasers
  • DEA, MEA, and TEA compounds
  • Synthetic dyes and colorants
  • Animal-derived ingredients (100% vegan)
  • Any ingredient not fully disclosed on label

Crown Scholars Program

Each year, we fund full-ride scholarships for ten aspiring cosmetologists from underserved communities. Our Crown Scholars receive tuition coverage, a complete CrownKinetics professional kit, mentorship from industry veterans, and guaranteed placement support upon graduation. Since 2020, we have graduated 35 Crown Scholars who now serve their communities with excellence.

Roots & Wisdom Initiative

In partnership with schools across Atlanta, Houston, Detroit, and Brooklyn, we lead workshops that teach young people the science and cultural history of Black hair. These sessions cover hair biology, ancestral care traditions, and self-affirmation — ensuring the next generation grows up celebrating their crowns rather than hiding them.

Fair Trade Partnerships

Our direct partnerships with women-led cooperatives in Ghana, Senegal, Jamaica, and Nigeria guarantee fair wages that exceed market rates by at least 40%. We also invest in community infrastructure including clean water access, school supplies, and healthcare resources for cooperative families.

Crown Confidence Campaign

Our annual Crown Confidence campaign partners with therapists and wellness practitioners to address the emotional impact of hair discrimination. We provide free resources on healing from texturism and colorism, host community healing circles, and advocate for CROWN Act legislation in every state where it has not yet been passed.
