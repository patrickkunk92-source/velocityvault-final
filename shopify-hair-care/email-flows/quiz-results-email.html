{{ hair_porosity|default:"Low" }} Porosity Tips

{% if hair_porosity == "Low" %} Your cuticles lay flat, making it harder for moisture to penetrate but great at retaining it once it does. Use lightweight, water-based products. Apply products to warm, damp hair. Use a hooded dryer or steamer during deep conditioning to help open those cuticles. Avoid heavy oils and butters that sit on top of your hair. {% elif hair_porosity == "High" %} Your cuticles are more open, allowing moisture in easily but also losing it quickly. Layer products using the LOC or LCO method to lock moisture in. Incorporate protein treatments every 4-6 weeks to strengthen the hair shaft. Rinse with cool water to help close cuticles. Rich butters and heavier oils are your allies. {% else %} Your normal porosity means your hair absorbs and retains moisture well. You have the most flexibility with products. Maintain your balance with regular conditioning and a consistent routine. Alternate between protein and moisture treatments based on how your hair feels. {% endif %}

{{ hair_density|default:"Medium" }} Density Care

{% if hair_density == "Fine" %} Fine-density hair needs volume without weight. Use lightweight leave-ins and avoid layering too many products. Focus on root-lifting techniques and volumizing styles. Less is more -- one to two styling products maximum. {% elif hair_density == "Thick" %} Thick-density hair can handle richer products and more layering. Section hair thoroughly during wash days to ensure product reaches every strand. Do not be afraid of generous product application -- your hair can take it. Deep conditioning is essential to keep all those strands hydrated. {% else %} Medium density gives you the best of both worlds. You can use a moderate amount of product without worrying about weight or under-coverage. Adjust product amounts based on the season and your current style. {% endif %}

Pro Tips for Type {{ hair_type|default:"4B" }} Hair

1

Wash Day Prep: Detangle with a wide-tooth comb and conditioner before shampooing. Work in small sections from ends to roots.

2

The LOC Method: Layer Liquid (water or leave-in), Oil (to seal), then Cream (to lock it all in). This is the golden rule for maximum moisture retention.

3

Refresh Between Washes: Spritz hair lightly with water and a touch of leave-in conditioner to revive curls and reduce dryness mid-week.

4

Night Routine: Pineapple your hair (loose high ponytail) or braid loosely, then cover with a satin bonnet. Your morning self will thank you.

5

Trim Regularly: Every 8-12 weeks, trim your ends. Healthy ends mean less tangling, less breakage, and better length retention.
