The SE Bikes Story

Founded in 1977 by Rich Menees and Pierre Labricole, SE Bikes didn't just enter the BMX world ‚Äî they created it. From the legendary Big Ripper cruiser to pro-grade freestyle machines, SE Bikes has been the heartbeat of street culture for nearly five decades.

Every frame tells a story. Every model carries the DNA of innovation. Every rider who chooses SE joins a legacy of riders who pushed boundaries, broke barriers, and defined what BMX could be.

Innovation Timeline

1977: Birth of BMX

Rich Menees and Pierre Labricole founded SE Bikes with a vision to craft the ultimate street bikes.

1980s: The Golden Era

SE Bikes became synonymous with freestyle. The Quadangle frame revolutionized bike design.

1990s: The Big Ripper

The iconic Big Ripper changed cruiser culture forever. Wheelies would never be the same.

2000s: Global Domination

SE Bikes became the choice of pro riders worldwide. From street to park to racing, SE dominated.

2020s: Modern Masters

Today, SE Bikes continues innovating. Blending heritage with cutting-edge design and materials.

What is BMX?

BMX stands for Bicycle Motocross ‚Äî but it's so much more than that. It's a culture. A lifestyle. A form of expression. BMX bikes are small, agile, and purpose-built for tricks, stunts, racing, and cruising. With 20-inch wheels (mostly), single-speed or multi-speed options, and reinforced frames, BMX bikes let you push creative boundaries and defy gravity.

Whether you're mastering wheelies, landing tricks, dominating the park, or cruising the streets, BMX is about freedom, progression, and community.
